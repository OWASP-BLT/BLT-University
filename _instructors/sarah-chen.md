Dr. Sarah Chen is a renowned cryptography researcher and web security expert with over 15 years of experience in information security. She holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from MIT, where her research focused on applied cryptography and secure systems design.

## Professional Background

Sarah has worked with major technology companies including Google and Microsoft, where she led security architecture teams and conducted security assessments of large-scale systems. She has discovered and responsibly disclosed numerous vulnerabilities in popular web applications and protocols.

## Expertise Areas

- Applied Cryptography
- Web Application Security
- Secure Systems Design
- Security Architecture
- Vulnerability Research

## Teaching Philosophy

"Security is everyone's responsibility. My goal is to make complex security concepts accessible to developers and help them build secure systems from the ground up. I believe in hands-on learning and real-world examples."

## Achievements

- Published 20+ peer-reviewed security papers
- Featured speaker at Black Hat, DEF CON, and RSA Conference
- OWASP Top Contributor (2020-2023)
- Author of "Modern Web Security" (O'Reilly, 2022)
- Bug bounty earnings: $500,000+

## Courses

Sarah teaches multiple courses at BLT University focusing on web security and cryptography, bringing her industry experience and research insights to every lesson.
