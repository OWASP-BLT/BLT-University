Become an Instructor

Share your cybersecurity expertise with our community

Why Teach at BLT University?

  • Share your knowledge with a global audience
  • Build your reputation in the cybersecurity community
  • Collaborate with other security professionals
  • Help make security education accessible to everyone
  • Keep your skills sharp by teaching others

How to Create a Course

1

Fork the Repository

Start by forking the BLT-University repository on GitHub.

2

Create Your Instructor Profile

Add a new file in the _instructors directory:

---
layout: instructor
title: Your Name
expertise: Your Area of Expertise
avatar: /assets/images/instructors/yourname.jpg
github: yourusername
linkedin: yourprofile
---

Your bio and teaching philosophy goes here...
3

Create Your Course

Add a new file in the _courses directory:

---
layout: course
title: Your Course Title
category: Course Category
level: Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced
duration: 4 weeks
instructor: Your Name
featured: false
prerequisites: Any prerequisites
description: Short course description

resources:
  - title: Introduction Video
    url: https://youtube.com/watch?v=...
    type: video
  - title: Course Slides
    url: /assets/courses/yourcourse/slides.pdf
    type: pdf

syllabus:
  - title: Week 1 - Introduction
    description: Course overview
    topics:
      - Topic 1
      - Topic 2
  - title: Week 2 - Deep Dive
    description: Advanced concepts
    topics:
      - Topic 3
      - Topic 4
---

# Course Content

Your detailed course content goes here using Markdown...

## Module 1: Introduction

Content for module 1...

## Module 2: Advanced Topics

Content for module 2...
4

Submit a Pull Request

Once your course is ready, submit a pull request using the course submission template. Our team will review and provide feedback.

5

Course Goes Live

After approval, your course will be merged and automatically published to the BLT University website!

Course Guidelines

  • Focus on Cybersecurity: All courses should be related to information security
  • Clear Structure: Organize content with clear learning objectives
  • Practical Examples: Include hands-on exercises and real-world scenarios
  • Multiple Formats: Combine videos, documents, and interactive content
  • Prerequisites: Clearly state any required prior knowledge
  • Open License: All content must be compatible with AGPL-3.0
